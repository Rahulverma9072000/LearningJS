A closure happens when a function remembers variable from outer scope , even after that outer scope is finished executing . Closures are one of JavaScript's most powerfull features .
 
 
Why are Closures Useful ?
 
1) Encapsulation : Closures let you hide variables and fucntions inside other functions , exposing only what you need . 
2) Data Persistence : Closures allow a function to "remember" the state of variables , even after it finishes running .
